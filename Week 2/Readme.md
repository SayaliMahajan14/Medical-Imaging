Week 2: Statistical Priors and Image Denoising

This week focused on statistical image modeling and image denoising techniques used in medical imaging. Concepts such as Random Fields, Markov Random Fields (MRFs), Gibbs distributions, and the Hammersley–Clifford theorem were studied to understand how prior information can be incorporated into image analysis. Different image priors, including Gaussian MRFs, Ising and Potts models, and edge-preserving priors, were explored. Various noise models encountered in medical imaging, such as Gaussian, Poisson, Rician, Rayleigh, and Compound Poisson noise, were also reviewed. The study further covered Bayesian denoising through MAP estimation, Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR), Total Variation (TV) regularization, and Non-Local Means denoising, providing an overview of both probabilistic and optimization-based approaches for image restoration.
