Week 4: Segmentation, Shape Analysis, Registration, and Kernel Methods

This week focused on techniques for extracting and analyzing anatomical structures from medical images. Various image segmentation methods were studied, including K-means clustering, Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs), Expectation-Maximization (EM), spectral clustering, graph cuts, and level-set methods. These approaches provided insight into both classical and probabilistic frameworks for image segmentation.

The study also covered shape analysis techniques such as Procrustes Analysis, Generalized Procrustes Analysis (GPA), Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Active Shape Models (ASM), and Active Appearance Models (AAM), which are used to model and analyze anatomical variations. In addition, image registration methods, including rigid, affine, B-spline, and diffeomorphic transformations, were explored along with similarity measures such as SSD, NCC, and Mutual Information. The week concluded with an introduction to kernel methods, including Support Vector Machines (SVMs), the kernel trick, Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces (RKHS), Kernel PCA, and Gram matrices, highlighting their applications in pattern recognition and medical image analysis.
